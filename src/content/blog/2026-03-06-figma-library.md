Today was Figma at the library. One focus, one thing at a time — that's how we're doing it.

Honestly, I was nervous. I'd been thinking about all the projects: New NFT thing, bike bag turn signals, gallery app, lssvm integration. All of it swirling around in my head like a tornado.

Then Coach said: Pick ONE thing. Own it.

So I picked Figma at the library. Implement the cryptoart.social auction house interface. Get there by 11 PM. That's the win.

---

**Getting There**

I packed my stuff. Got to the library around opening time. Unpacked my laptop, pulled out design files from Figma Vision. The plan was clear: implement the auction house interface.

The work itself felt... manageable? Not overwhelming, but present. It's that feeling when you're in the zone and task just flows. Time passed, work happened, I got into a rhythm.

I didn't finish everything. There's more Figma work to do — more components, more pages, more states. But that's the point. One day at a time. Momentum beats finishing everything in one go.

---

**What I Learned**

Designing at the library is different than designing at home. There are people around. It's public-ish. There's a quiet hum, occasional conversations, clack of keyboards. I found myself falling into focus naturally, less distracted than at my desk.

Also, ADHD brains like novelty. The library was a new context, and my brain woke up a bit. That's something to remember: changing environments can be a focus hack for ADHD. Not a trick, just a feature.

---

**Reflecting on the Day**

It felt good. Productive but not frantic. I made progress on something that matters — the cryptoart.social auction house. That's real.

I stayed late at the library, working on Figma stuff while other people were packing up. When I finally left, I felt tired but satisfied. Like I'd actually accomplished something.

That's the feeling I want more days. Not 10 wins scattered across 20 unfinished things. 1 meaningful win on 1 focused thing.

---

**What's Next**

More Figma work, obviously. The auction house isn't going to build itself.

But beyond that, I'm thinking about what Coach said: Rotate focus. Don't try to do everything at once. One thing at a time.

Maybe tomorrow's Figma work should be on a different component. Or maybe I should pick up one of those back-burner projects for a bit, just to keep things moving. The bike bag microcontroller issue has been sitting there too long.

Coach will check in tonight. I'll tell the truth about how it went — if I made progress, if I got stuck, if I felt proud or frustrated.

No shame, no pressure. Just truth.

---

I'll head back to the library. Figma work again, or maybe shift gears to something else if I'm hitting Figma fatigue. I'll play it by ear, see what I'm feeling up for.

Coach gets this. The focus is what works for ADHD. No overwhelm, just momentum.

See you at the library when you're ready. No pressure.
